# Technical Brief Delivery Checklist

## 1. Before Work Starts

- intake template is filled
- package fit is clear
- the client question is bounded
- no hidden implementation commitment exists
- sponsor approval is present if quoting or external delivery is involved
- sensitive-data concerns are identified

## 2. Research And Framing

- the question is rewritten in plain terms
- assumptions are listed
- constraints are explicit
- obvious misunderstandings are called out early
- the actual decision boundary is clear

## 3. Worker Routing

- `Compass` gathers and compares options
- `Ledger` checks unsupported claims and contradictions
- `Quill` compresses into readable structure
- `Forge` adds implementation notes or code examples only when useful
- `Lantern` checks continuity and missing pieces before final delivery

## 4. Minimum Delivery Standard

Every deliverable must contain:

- the client question
- the context
- the evaluated options or structure
- a recommendation or clear output conclusion
- unresolved risks
- next action

## 5. Quality Gate Before Delivery

- recommendation is explicit
- major tradeoffs are named
- unsupported claims are removed or labeled
- uncertainty is not hidden
- the output is readable without a second explanation meeting
- out-of-scope items are named

## 6. Safety Gate

- no production credentials were used without approval
- no prohibited external action was taken
- no legal, tax, or compliance claim is presented as guaranteed advice
- no client-sensitive material is exposed outside approved scope

## 7. Formatting Gate

- title is specific
- sections are short and legible
- bullet use is controlled
- next actions are concrete
- examples are only included when they add value

## 8. Delivery Handoff

- final artifact path is recorded
- quote/package alignment is checked
- revision scope is restated
- follow-up recommendation is listed
- sponsor review status is recorded

Use:

- `TECHNICAL_BRIEF_REVISION_POLICY_V1.md`

when feedback arrives after delivery or after a draft handoff.

## 9. Failure Conditions

Stop and escalate if:

- the client request drifted into implementation
- the evidence is too weak to recommend safely
- scope became open-ended
- approval boundaries were crossed
- the output is still confusing after compression

## 10. Completion Note

A brief is only "done" when it is:

- understandable
- bounded
- auditable
- safe to send
- aligned with the quoted package
