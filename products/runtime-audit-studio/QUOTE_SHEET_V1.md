# Runtime Audit Studio Quote Sheet V1

## 1. Purpose

This is the sponsor-facing internal quote guide for `Runtime Audit Studio`.

It is not an automatic public price list.

Use it to decide what to quote before any external commitment is made.

## 2. Quote Rules

- keep scope bounded
- quote the smallest honest package
- do not smuggle implementation work into an audit package
- if sensitive access or production action is required, stop and reframe

## 3. Standard Packages

### Package A: Snapshot Runtime Audit

- ideal scope: one current runtime snapshot
- deliverable: one audit pack
- turnaround target: 1 to 2 business days
- internal quote band: `8,000 JPY` to `15,000 JPY`

Use this when:

- the main need is a current-state health readout
- the client wants one recommendation and one next-step list
- evidence already exists in files and logs

### Package B: Comparative Runtime Audit

- ideal scope: compare two operating approaches or two snapshots
- deliverable: one audit pack with compare section
- turnaround target: 2 to 4 business days
- internal quote band: `15,000 JPY` to `30,000 JPY`

Use this when:

- the client is deciding between two baselines
- the runtime evidence is split across alternative approaches
- the client needs a bounded compare-and-recommend deliverable

### Package C: Follow-Up Audit

- ideal scope: one re-audit after a specific correction cycle
- deliverable: delta-focused follow-up audit
- turnaround target: 1 to 3 business days
- internal quote band: `6,000 JPY` to `12,000 JPY`

Use this when:

- a prior audit already exists
- the client wants to know if a fix actually improved the system
- the scope is explicitly "before vs after"

## 4. Quote Modifiers

Increase price when:

- logs are messy or incomplete
- the environment is hybrid or multi-host
- the client wants both executive and technical wording
- the evidence requires manual reconciliation across multiple sources

Keep price lower when:

- the evidence is already well organized
- the system is single-host and local-first
- the question is narrow
- the turnaround is flexible

## 5. Do Not Quote Under This Product If

- the client actually wants implementation
- the client wants emergency live debugging
- the client needs privileged production access
- the client expects a compliance or legal certification
- the client expects guaranteed performance claims

In those cases:

- reframe
- split
- or decline

## 6. Sponsor Review Checklist Before Sending

- package fit is correct
- scope is bounded
- evidence is sufficient for an evidence-first review
- no hidden implementation commitment exists
- no public claim exceeds the actual audit evidence
- turnaround is realistic
