# Runtime Audit Studio Marketplace Listing

## Listing headline
Runtime Audit for local LLM, Codex, and Claude-style automation setups

## Short platform subtitle
I inspect your AI runtime as it actually runs, then tell you what is healthy, what is brittle, and what should be fixed first.

## Core listing copy
Most AI workflow setups look fine in demos and break in long-running use.

Northbridge Systems audits local LLM and agentic runtime setups with an operations-first lens. I do not sell "AI magic." I read the evidence your system already produces: supervisor state, heartbeat, queue behavior, local model health, runtime logs, and approval boundaries.

You get a bounded audit pack that explains:

- what is healthy right now
- what is brittle or misleading
- where autonomy is safe
- where approval gates should stay
- what to fix first if you want a more reliable system

This is a good fit if you are running or designing:

- Ollama or other local-model workflows
- Codex / Claude-style coding agents
- background job loops, supervisors, or schedulers
- multi-worker AI setups
- memory, retry, or recovery logic

## What the client receives
- A runtime audit report with findings, risk level, and recommended next actions.
- A short operational summary that can be shown to a sponsor or decision-maker.
- A bounded scope: I say what I checked, what I did not check, and what still needs live confirmation.

## First package recommendation
Start with the Snapshot Runtime Audit.

It is the best first engagement because it is fast, evidence-first, and does not require handing over control of your system.

## Package framing
### Snapshot Runtime Audit
- Best for: a first look at one environment
- Outcome: one audit pack with findings and priorities

### Comparative Runtime Audit
- Best for: comparing two versions, branches, or operating modes
- Outcome: side-by-side recommendation with tradeoffs

### Follow-Up Audit
- Best for: checking whether fixes actually improved the system
- Outcome: short validation report with updated risk view

## What I need from the client
- A short description of the runtime and the goal.
- Access to relevant state, logs, or exported artifacts.
- The current failure mode or concern, if one exists.
- Clear boundaries on what is in scope.

## What this service is not
- I do not promise full implementation.
- I do not claim that a passing demo means the system is production ready.
- I do not remove approval boundaries just to make the system look more autonomous.
- I do not package speculation as evidence.

## Tone and positioning
Northbridge Systems is for people who want an honest technical read, not a hype-heavy AI pitch.

If your system is healthy, I will say so.
If it is fragile, I will say that too.
