## 4. Review Questions

### Decision 1: Is the profile accurate?

- recommended answer:
  - approve
- check:
  - does it match the real service line?
  - does it avoid exaggerated capability claims?

### Decision 2: Is the boundary language strong enough?

- recommended answer:
  - approve
- check:
  - does it clearly avoid implying deployment, production access, or open-ended implementation?

### Decision 3: Are the keywords and fit examples useful?

- recommended answer:
  - approve
- check:
  - do the keywords match likely buyer language?
  - do the best-fit examples stay within current company strength?

### Decision 4: Can it be edited for profile/platform formatting?

- recommended answer:
  - approve
- allowed edits:
  - shortening
  - keyword reshuffling
  - headline adjustment
  - light wording cleanup
- not allowed without fresh review:
  - adding new service claims
  - implying implementation ownership
  - changing the bounded-first-engagement posture

### Decision 5: Is it approved for immediate publication?

- recommended answer:
  - no
- reason:
  - current operating rule still keeps final public posting sponsor-gated

## 5. Suggested Publication Rule

Safe rule:

- the draft may be treated as sponsor-reviewed working copy
- the exact profile version and platform destination still require sponsor confirmation

## 6. Risk Notes

- profile pages can drift into inflated positioning more easily than operational docs
- keyword editing can accidentally over-widen the offer
- profile summaries can hide the implementation boundary if shortened too aggressively
