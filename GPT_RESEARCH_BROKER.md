# GPT Research Broker

## 1. Purpose

The sponsor may act as a research broker by obtaining outside research through GPT or another external system.

This document standardizes how the companies should ask for that help.

## 2. When to Use

Use the sponsor as a research broker when:

- a fact is missing
- a current market check is needed
- a vendor comparison is needed
- a legal or policy question needs source gathering
- outside synthesis would save meaningful time

Do not use the broker for questions that can be answered locally with existing records.

## 3. Good Request Shape

A good broker request is:

- narrow
- source-aware
- outcome-oriented
- time-bounded

Bad request:

- "Research local LLMs."

Good request:

- "Compare current 2026 Windows-friendly local inference stacks for RTX 4070 Ti SUPER, focusing on tool use, quantized model support, and VRAM efficiency."

## 4. Required Fields

Every broker request should include:

- requesting company
- exact question
- why the answer matters
- preferred source type
- desired answer format
- urgency

## 5. Preferred Uses by Company

`Northbridge Systems` should use brokered research for:

- systems comparisons
- benchmark validation
- hardware planning
- security or compliance references

The `Southgate Research` should use brokered research for:

- strategy comparisons
- market analysis
- product positioning
- synthesis of external options

## 6. Output Standard

Brokered research should come back in one of these forms:

- short answer
- decision memo
- comparison table
- risk brief
- implementation note

## 7. Follow-Up Rule

If the first answer is incomplete, the companies should send a tighter follow-up question instead of repeating the original one broadly.
