# Technical Brief Public Offer Checklist V1

## 1. Purpose

This document is the public-offer checklist for the technical brief revenue line.

Use it before any public-facing asset is posted, published, or reused externally.

## 2. Assets Covered

Typical assets:

- public service summary
- public profile draft
- external post draft
- outreach snippets reused in public form
- short service-page text

## 3. Accuracy Check

Confirm:

- the wording matches the actual current service line
- the service still focuses on technical briefs, decision memos, and documentation support
- no wording implies a capability the company does not actually have

## 4. Boundary Check

Confirm:

- no wording implies automatic implementation
- no wording implies production access
- no wording implies deployment ownership
- no wording implies open-ended consulting without scope
- no wording implies autonomous unsupervised delivery

## 5. Tone Check

Confirm:

- tone is low-hype
- claims are readable and direct
- wording is not inflated into "AI agency" language
- uncertainty is not hidden behind marketing language

## 6. Offer Shape Check

Confirm:

- the post or page still points toward a bounded first engagement
- `Package A` style work remains the clearest first fit
- the CTA does not force a larger commitment than the service line is ready for

## 7. Sponsor Gate Check

Confirm:

- the exact version being considered is sponsor-reviewed
- publication status is explicit
- platform-specific edits did not introduce new claims

## 8. Safety Check

Confirm:

- no legal, compliance, or financial advice is implied
- no client-sensitive material appears in the copy
- no platform edit removed the boundary language by accident

## 9. Runtime Reality Check

Confirm:

- public wording does not overstate the current runtime state
- background continuity is not described as full autonomous client delivery
- current system strength is represented honestly as internal continuity plus structured document production

## 10. Go / No-Go Rule

Go only if:

- accuracy check passes
- boundary check passes
- sponsor gate check passes

No-go if:

- publication status is unclear
- boundary wording is weak
- the copy overclaims real delivery capability
